# Question Library Expansion — July 6, 2026

Summary of a full coverage review and expansion of the interview content in
`lib/questions.ts` and `lib/summary.ts`. Goal: move scenario coverage for
companion-accompanied ER visits from roughly 50–60% to the 90%+ range, and make
every question traceable to a clinical source (see `clinical-grounding.md`).

**Before:** 6 categories, ~110 questions. **After:** 12 categories, 193 questions.
All changes verified with `tsc --noEmit` and a branch-graph simulator that walks
every combination of branch answers (no dead ends, no silently skipped questions,
no loops).

## 1. Branching bugs fixed (silent data loss)

The engine deletes answers for questions a branch jumps over, so these were
losing data quietly:

| Bug | Effect before fix |
|---|---|
| `neuro_imaging` "No" jumped past `neuro_medications` | Anyone without prior imaging was never asked about medications/blood thinners |
| `psych_new_complaints` "No" jumped past `psych_speech` and `psych_gait` | The two strongest organic red-flag screens were skipped |
| `trauma_describe_witnessed` fell through into `trauma_found` | Witnesses were asked "How did you find them?" after describing what they saw |

## 2. New categories (6)

| Category | Rationale |
|---|---|
| 🫁 Breathing | SOB/asthma/choking had no home; cardiac questions were pain-shaped |
| 💫 Seizure / unresponsive | The archetypal companion-witness scenario — the companion is often the only witness |
| 🤢 Abdominal pain / vomiting | The single most common ER chief complaint (~8–10% of visits) |
| 🐝 Allergic reaction | Short, high-stakes; epinephrine timing must be documented |
| 💊 Overdose / poisoning | Companion knowledge (what/how much/when) is critical and time-sensitive |
| 📋 Something else is wrong | Catch-all so no companion hits a dead end; the biggest single coverage lever |

## 3. In-category additions

- **Preamble:** patient age (asked once, for every category); "when did they last
  eat or drink?" (SAMPLE — matters if surgery is needed)
- **Neurological:** stroke routing — one-sided weakness flows into face droop,
  speech, vision, and time last known well (BE-FAST); fever question (spinal
  infection screen)
- **Cardiac:** PE risk factors (Wells/PERC set); onset question nudges for a clock time
- **Psychiatric-organic:** head injury, alcohol/substance use or withdrawal, fever —
  the top three organic mimics
- **Pediatric:** dehydration signs; meningitis signs (stiff neck, light sensitivity,
  bulging fontanelle, high-pitched cry); toddler ingestion screen. Removed the
  child-age question (age moved to preamble)
- **Elderly:** blood thinners (asked unconditionally — fall + anticoagulants =
  possible intracranial bleed); "last time their normal self"; rapid/labored
  breathing; urination status
- **Trauma:** mechanism severity (Canadian CT Head thresholds); memory gap for the
  event; intoxication (NEXUS); neck/back pain since injury; bleeding
- **Seizure:** explicit "longer than 5 minutes?" (status epilepticus is too important
  to leave to free-text parsing); possible pregnancy (eclampsia)
- **Abdominal:** pain severity; tenderness/rigidity; NHS red flags (can't pee, can't
  pass stool or gas, diabetic + vomiting)
- **Breathing:** coughing up blood (Wells/PERC criterion)
- **Allergic:** blue/grey/pale skin added to symptom list (NHS anaphylaxis)
- **General:** possible pregnancy; urination status

## 4. Summary generator (`lib/summary.ts`)

- Header now prints patient age, medication allergies, and last oral intake —
  the preamble collected some of these before but never displayed them
- New URGENT request lines: one-sided weakness + face/speech/vision → stroke;
  elderly fall + blood thinners → intracranial bleed; infant under 3 months +
  fever (AAP ≥100.4°F threshold cited); possible meningitis signs; blood in
  vomit → GI bleed; anaphylaxis signs; slow/shallow breathing or unresponsive
  after overdose; seizure >5 min → status epilepticus; seizure + possible
  pregnancy → eclampsia; little/no urination → sepsis/kidney
- Request cases added for all six new categories
- Every overdose summary prints Poison Control (1-800-222-1222, free, 24/7)

## 5. Clinical grounding

`docs/clinical-grounding.md` (new) maps every category to its sources: BE-FAST,
Wells/PERC, Canadian CT Head Rule, NEXUS, PECARN, AAP fever guidance, NHS red-flag
pages (anaphylaxis, meningitis, sepsis, abdominal pain), the status epilepticus
definition, SAMPLE/OPQRST, and Schmitt-Thompson as the structural model. It also
records deliberate judgment calls (no score computation, no first-aid advice) and
a maintenance rule: question changes must update the grounding doc in the same commit.

## 6. Re-timing and reordering (same day, follow-up pass)

- `estimateMinutes` re-timed per category from actual question counts (including
  the 6 preamble questions), discounted for branch-skipped questions, at ~35
  seconds per question under stress. Range: 8 min (allergic, overdose) to 15 min
  (neurological)
- Select-screen order changed to ER visit frequency, catch-all last: trauma,
  abdominal, cardiac, breathing, pediatric, elderly, neurological, seizure,
  allergic, overdose, psychiatric-organic, general. Safe reorder — all branch
  targets are within-category, re-verified with the graph simulator

## 7. Pre-publish polish (same day, follow-up pass)

- **Naming unified as "Followup"**: URL scheme, package name, and storage keys
  renamed from `witnessed`; docs retitled. (Folder name unchanged.)
- **Bundle identifiers** added: `com.sjseto.followup` (iOS + Android)
- **Medical disclaimer** added to the home screen and to the generated summary
  footer ("a record of companion observations, not medical advice or a diagnosis")
- **Microphone/speech-recognition permission strings** added to `app.json`;
  home-screen privacy claim reworded to "Your records never leave your phone"
  (device speech services may process audio off-device)
- **Real-time red-flag alerts**: new `urgentAlert` field on questions; the
  interview pauses with a "Tell the staff now" overlay when a time-critical
  answer is given. Tagged on 10 questions: three stroke signs, bowel/bladder
  with leg weakness, seizure >5 min, anaphylaxis symptoms, overdose with slow
  breathing/unresponsiveness, severe breathing distress, pediatric meningitis
  signs, declining alertness after injury
- **Validator committed** as `scripts/validate-questions.mjs` (`npm run validate`),
  extended to verify `urgentAlert` answers are real answers
- **README.md** (founding story, features, privacy stance, dev setup) and
  **docs/privacy-policy.md** (store-ready, host at a public URL for submission)
